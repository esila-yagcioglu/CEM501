### Week 4 Presentation Reflection

The strongest aspect of my presentation was the clarity and structure of the message, especially the use of a strong opening hook. I immediately introduced the critical issue — a 14-day delay on the project — and clearly stated that decisions were required during the meeting. This aligns with the “hook effectiveness” principle discussed in class, as it captured attention early and established urgency. The presentation followed a clear problem–solution–action structure, which made the content easy to follow and aligned with effective communication principles.

Since I could not attend the live session and receive peer feedback, I watched my video again to give feedback to myself. One important observation was related to my delivery. At the beginning of the presentation, I appeared slightly restless, as I was moving my foot unconsciously for a few seconds. Although it stabilized shortly after, it affected my initial confidence. In addition, I noticed that I mispronounced a few words during the presentation, and in some technical parts, I spoke slightly faster than intended. These factors could reduce clarity and professionalism from the audience’s perspective.

For my next presentation, I will take a specific and measurable action: I will rehearse the full presentation at least three times with a timer and record myself once before presenting. While reviewing the recording, I will focus specifically on body language and pronunciation. I will also pause for 1–2 seconds after key points to control my pacing and improve clarity. This approach will help me deliver the content more confidently and professionally.

Before presenting, I would rate my nervousness as a 7 out of 10. I felt some pressure to deliver the information clearly and effectively. I mainly used preparation as a strategy by practicing the script multiple times, which helped reduce uncertainty. Although I did not actively use breathing techniques, repeated practice increased my confidence and allowed me to manage my nervousness better during the presentation.
